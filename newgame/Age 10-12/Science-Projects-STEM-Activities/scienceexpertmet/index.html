Fun Science Experiments

Easy Home Experiments

These simple experiments use common household items to demonstrate scientific principles.

Volcano Eruption

Create a chemical reaction with baking soda and vinegar.

Materials:

  • Baking soda
  • Vinegar
  • Dish soap
  • Food coloring
  • Small bottle

Steps:

  1. Place the bottle on a tray.
  2. Add 2 tbsp baking soda, 1 tsp dish soap and food coloring.
  3. Pour in vinegar and watch the eruption!

The acid (vinegar) and base (baking soda) react to produce carbon dioxide gas, creating the eruption.

Rainbow Milk

Explore surface tension with milk, food coloring and soap.

Materials:

  • Milk (whole works best)
  • Food coloring
  • Dish soap
  • Cotton swabs
  • Shallow dish

Steps:

  1. Pour milk into the dish to cover the bottom.
  2. Add drops of different food coloring.
  3. Dip cotton swab in soap and touch the milk.
  4. Watch the colors swirl!

Soap breaks the surface tension of milk and reacts with fat molecules, causing the movement.

Balloon Rocket

Demonstrate Newton's Third Law of Motion.

Materials:

  • Balloon
  • String
  • Straw
  • Tape

Steps:

  1. Thread string through straw and tie between two chairs.
  2. Blow up balloon (don't tie) and tape to straw.
  3. Let go and watch it zoom!

Air rushing out pushes the balloon forward (action) which creates an equal opposite force (reaction).
